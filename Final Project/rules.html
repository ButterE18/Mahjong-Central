Base Rules

Mahjong is a 4-player game of skill, strategy, and calculation — involving a degree of chance. The goal is to be the first player to complete a legal hand using drawn and discarded tiles.

  • Each player starts with 13 tiles.
  • Players take turns drawing and discarding tiles.
  • The winning hand consists of 4 melds and a pair (unless using special hands).
  • Points are awarded based on hand value and winning method (drawn or discard).

Chinese Mahjong

Chinese Mahjong has many variations, but typically emphasizes higher-point hands and often uses a points-based payout system.

  • Common hands include Pungs (three of a kind) and Chows (sequences).
  • Flowers and Seasons tiles provide bonus points.
  • Winning may require a minimum point threshold (such as 8 points).
  • Scoring is based on patterns, bonus tiles, and method of winning.
  • Self-draw wins often score extra points.

Resource: Chinese Mahjong Wikipedia

Riichi Mahjong

Riichi Mahjong (Japanese style) is characterized by its emphasis on declared ready hands ("Riichi") and structured scoring using Yaku (hand patterns).

  • Declaring "Riichi" locks your hand and allows bonus points.
  • Winning hands must have at least one Yaku (valid hand type).
  • Special moves include "Ippatsu" (winning within one round after Riichi) and "Dora" bonuses.
  • Penalty points exist for invalid hands ("Chombo").
  • Scoring is strictly calculated with Han and Fu values.
